Essentials of Innovation and Entrepreneurship (EIE) 
In an era defined by rapid technological advancements (e.g., Generative AI, Machine Learning), environmental challenges (e.g., climate change, sustainability), and economic shifts, entrepreneurial thinking is essential for success in any field. EIE empowers you to:
Identify and seize opportunities in dynamic markets.

What is EIE?
EIE is a two-part, pan-university course designed to foster an innovation and entrepreneurship (I&E) mindset:
Part I (3rd Semester, 2 credits): Introduces foundational concepts, including:
Entrepreneurial mindset and opportunity identification.
Business acumen (market analysis, financial basics).
Soft skills (communication, collaboration, problem-solving).
Part II (4th Semester, 2 credits): Builds on Part I with advanced topics:
Customer discovery and validation.
Prototyping and digital business models.
Scaling strategies and legal fundamentals.
The course integrates the CIE Ignite Ideathon, a hands-on startup challenge where students develop and pitch ideas, gaining real-world experience and mentorship.

## EIE Part 2
Essentials of Innovation and Entrepreneurship (EIE) Part II at PES University
Welcome to Essentials of Innovation and Entrepreneurship (EIE) Part II, a 2-credit course offered in the 4th semester at PES University. Spearheaded by the Centre for Innovation and Entrepreneurship (CIE), this pan-university program builds on EIE Part I, deepening students’ entrepreneurial mindset and skills. Open to students from all disciplines, EIE Part II integrates the CIE Ignite Ideathon and features insights from startup founders and industry experts, preparing participants for dynamic careers in startups, corporations, or small businesses.
Why EIE Part II?
The global workforce is rapidly evolving due to technological advancements (e.g., Generative AI, Machine Learning), environmental challenges (e.g., climate change, sustainability), and economic shifts. EIE Part II equips students to:
Identify and seize opportunities in dynamic markets.
Develop innovative solutions and navigate the idea-to-market process.
Cultivate an innovation and entrepreneurship (I&E) mindset applicable to any career path.
As a pan-university course, EIE Part II fosters interdisciplinary collaboration, engaging students from Engineering, Design, Management, Law, and more, reflecting the diverse nature of real-world entrepreneurship.
Course Objectives
EIE Part II aims to:
Deepen students’ I&E mindset and practical skills for starting entrepreneurial ventures.
Enable exploration of I&E in contexts like startups, corporate entrepreneurship, and MSMEs.
Enhance understanding of transformative technologies and their entrepreneurial implications.
Build advanced business acumen, focusing on market research, financials, and legal fundamentals.
Strengthen soft skills (communication, collaboration, problem-solving) for life beyond university.
Learning Outcomes
By the end of EIE Part II, students will:
Apply advanced I&E skills in real-world scenarios, including through the CIE Ignite Ideathon.
Master key elements of the idea-to-market process, with proficiency in Lean Canvas and business models.
Gain deeper awareness of technological transformations (e.g., Industry 4.0-5.0, AI-led digital transformation) and related career opportunities.
Develop a robust understanding of business fundamentals, including legal structures and digital marketing.
Connect with like-minded peers for potential collaborations in future I&E ventures.

## Product Management in the AI Era at PES University
Product Management in the AI Era at PES University
Welcome to Product Management in the AI Era, a 2-credit Special Topic course offered by the Centre for Innovation and Entrepreneurship (CIE) at PES University for 8th-semester students. This course introduces the critical skills of product management in the context of Artificial Intelligence (AI), empowering students to create impactful products that align technology, customer needs, and business goals. Open to all Engineering and Design students, it prepares participants for high-demand roles as Product Managers (PMs), often dubbed ‘mini-CEOs,’ in industries and startups worldwide.

Why Product Management in the AI Era?
In today’s AI-driven technological landscape, engineering and design students must go beyond technical expertise to understand user needs and business contexts. This course equips students to:
Develop high-quality products with market impact and revenue potential.
Think holistically about the product lifecycle, from ideation to launch.
Collaborate effectively in cross-functional teams to drive innovation.
By mastering product management fundamentals, students gain a competitive edge in industries seeking PMs who can navigate the complexities of AI-led transformations, making data-driven decisions to enhance user satisfaction and business success.
Course Objectives
Introduce core concepts of product management, including roles and responsibilities.
Equip students with skills in user research, market analysis, and product roadmap development.
Teach collaboration across engineering, design, and business teams using Agile methodologies.
Enable students to measure product success using metrics and develop go-to-market strategies.
Learning Outcomes
Upon completion, students will:
Understand and articulate the role of a product manager and the product lifecycle.
Conduct user and market research to inform product decisions and create product roadmaps.
Write product requirements and collaborate effectively with cross-functional teams.
Apply Agile methods, use product metrics, and develop go-to-market plans for product launches.
Course Structure
This 2-credit course is designed for 8th-semester students and delivered in a hybrid format, primarily online (evenings or Saturdays) with 3-4 in-person sessions (Saturdays, subject to availability). The pedagogy emphasizes project-based learning, case discussions, and a final product definition/pitch project.

PAML Train-the-Trainer Session Plans
Each session is designed to build both technical proficiency and teaching capabilities, with hands-on activities to reinforce learning.
Session 1: Introduction to AI, ML, DL, and Data Preparation
Topics:
Overview of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and its real-world applications.
Differences between AI, Machine Learning (ML), and Deep Learning (DL).
Data Preparation: Data types (structured, unstructured), handling missing values, outliers, cleaning, and transformation.
Activities: Hands-on Python exercises using pandas for dataset cleaning and preprocessing.
Objective: Establish a foundational understanding of AI, ML, and DL, and equip participants with practical data preparation skills.
Session 2: Classification and Regression
Topics:
Classification Techniques: Random Forest, XGBoost.
Regression Techniques: Linear, Ridge, Lasso.
Basics of model deployment for classification and regression.
Activities: Implement classification and regression models using scikit-learn.
Objective: Master core supervised learning techniques and understand model deployment basics.
Session 3: Data Processing and Feature Engineering
Topics:
Scaling, normalization, and encoding techniques.
Feature engineering and selection methods.
Train/test splits and cross-validation strategies.
Activities: Practical exercises to process data and create meaningful features.
Objective: Develop skills for effective data processing and feature engineering to enhance ML models.
Session 4: Vision and Neural Networks
Topics:
Neural Networks (NN) and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN).
YOLO and Vision Transformers for image-based tasks.
Activities: Train a CNN for image classification and explore object detection with YOLO.
Objective: Apply neural networks to computer vision tasks.
Session 5: Unsupervised Learning
Topics:
Clustering techniques: K-Means, DBSCAN.
Dimensionality reduction: PCA, t-SNE.
Activities: Hands-on clustering and dimensionality reduction using the MNIST dataset.
Objective: Learn techniques for analyzing and exploring unlabeled data.
Session 6: Sequential Models
Topics:
Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN).
GRU and LSTM for forecasting and time-series analysis.
Activities: Implement sequential models for time-series forecasting.
Objective: Understand and apply models for temporal data analysis.
Session 7: Basics of GANs and NLP
Topics:
Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs): Fundamentals and applications.
Natural Language Processing (NLP): Tokenization, text classification, introduction to BERT.
Activities: Implement a basic GAN for synthetic data generation and perform text classification with pre-trained NLP models.
Objective: Gain an introductory understanding of generative models and NLP techniques.

Below, explore our key success stories that showcase the strength of CIE’s industry ecosystem.
Intel Collaboration: Fast-Tracking AI/ML Solutions
Since 2018, Intel has partnered with CIE through the Capstone Project with Industry Partner (CPIP) initiative, fostering innovation in AI and machine learning. In 2024, five student teams, mentored by Intel experts, developed AI/ML solutions for Retail, FinTech, and Healthcare, delivering results in just six weeks—a process that typically takes months for Intel’s own teams. This remarkable efficiency highlights the caliber of PES University students and the value of industry mentorship.
In 2018, students from the Game Development Club leveraged their skills to accelerate Intel’s development timelines significantly, showcasing early success in this collaboration. Documented in the IEEE Educon 2021 paper, “India Industry-University Collaboration – A Novel Approach Combining Technology, Innovation, and Entrepreneurship,” this partnership exemplifies a model that integrates technology, innovation, and entrepreneurship for mutually rewarding outcomes.
Impact:
Delivered AI/ML projects in six weeks vs. months for Intel teams.
Enhanced student skills in AI/ML through hands-on industry mentorship.
Provided Intel with rapid prototyping and innovative solutions.
Cisco ThingQbator: Launching Student Startups
The Cisco ThingQbator program has been a catalyst for entrepreneurial talent at CIE. In Cohort 6, eight PES University teams ranked among the top 20 from over 1,050 applications nationwide. The team NeuRoar secured a top 10 position and ₹5 Lakh in incubation support for their assistive technology project, designed to support neurodivergent individuals. This achievement underscores CIE’s ability to nurture socially impactful startups.
Other startups from this cohort include:
GrowPal: A platform empowering small businesses.
Xperios: An app for hobby exploration.
QuickPick: A meal ordering solution.
Finn: A financial literacy platform.

These ventures demonstrate the diverse innovation fostered at CIE, addressing real-world challenges across multiple domains.
Impact:
NeuRoar secured ₹5 Lakh for assistive technology development.

Eight teams in top 20, showcasing PES University’s entrepreneurial talent.
CAVE Labs: Innovating with AR/VR Technologies
CAVE Labs, a CIE initiative, has pioneered AR/VR solutions through collaborations with industry partners like Infinity Engineering and Fabrik. Since November 2022, student teams have developed impactful applications, including:
Virtual Twin of a Drone: A training tool for pilots, enhancing safety and reducing costs.
VR Driving Simulator: Developed with Infinity Engineering for immersive driver training.
Nursing Education Software: Deployed at PESU EC campus to improve medical training.
Virtual BE Block Recreation: A VR showcase of architectural visualization.

Strengthened partnerships with startups and companies.
Greenifly: Scaling Agritech Innovation
Greenifly, an agritech startup, began as a capstone project under CIE’s mentorship and scaled into a funded venture with support from PESU Venture Labs (PVL). Specializing in multispectral and spray drones for NPK (nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium) analysis, Greenifly achieves 98-99% accuracy in agricultural diagnostics. The startup is now targeting global markets, including coffee, tea, and rice sectors, demonstrating CIE’s role in transforming student ideas into market-ready solutions.
Impact:
